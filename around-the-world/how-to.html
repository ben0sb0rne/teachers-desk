Around the World

The classic: the champion holds the seat, a challenger stands beside it. Pick a class, choose your question sets (multiplication, doubles, fractions — mix as many as you like; they shuffle into one pool), and enter the ring.

Ring the bell (or Space) and a flashcard shows a quick mental-math question. First to say the answer wins the round — tap the card (or Space) to flip it and check, then tap the winner's plate (or use the ← → keys). The champion's streak grows; a dethroned champion sits, and the next challenger steps in.

Seats work like the real game: when a champion falls, they sit in the seat the winner just left, and the walk carries on around the room. A champion who beats everyone sweeps the match.

Verbal mode runs the same ceremony while you ask your own questions, and Flashcards only hides the name plates entirely — just giant cards, for classes that run the walk themselves. End game crowns the Champion of the Room — longest streak takes the title, and class records stick around to be beaten.

Every round counts toward both students' participation tallies, same as a wheel spin.
