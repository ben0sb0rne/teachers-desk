FOUNDATIONAL FRACTIONS (5.NF / 6.NS.1)
============================================================

Standards live in elementary (5.NF) but high-leverage as
review — fluency gaps in fraction operations are the most
common upstream cause of struggle with 7.NS rational-number
work. Pulled back into year 1 for that reason.

  Simplifying Fractions
    Standard: 4.NF.1
    Reduce a fraction to lowest terms
    Notes: Numerators and denominators ≤ 24. Answers in
           lowest terms (e.g. 6/8 → 3/4).
    Variants:
      - Default        [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-4/simplifying-fractions.csv

  Adding & Subtracting Fractions
    Standard: 5.NF.1
    Unlike denominators; answers in simplest form
    Notes: Denominators ≤ 12. Improper-only answer format
           (e.g. 7/4 not 1 3/4) — locks the answer pool.
    Variants:
      - Addition only       [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-5/adding-fractions.csv
      - Subtraction only    [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-5/subtracting-fractions.csv
      - Mixed (recommended) [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-5/mixed-fractions.csv

  Multiplying & Dividing Fractions
    Standards: 5.NF.4 (multiply) / 5.NF.7 / 6.NS.1 (divide)
    Multiply: fraction × fraction. Divide: flip-and-multiply.
    Notes: Numerators/denominators ≤ 10. Improper-only
           answers in lowest terms. Division CSV excludes
           integer-valued answers (no 1/2 ÷ 1/4 = 2) to keep
           the answer pool format-consistent with the rest
           of the fraction set.
    Variants:
      - Multiplication only [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-5/multiplying-fractions.csv
      - Division only       [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-5/dividing-fractions.csv
      - Mixed (recommended) [SHIPPED]
          sets/grade-5/mixed-mult-div-fractions.csv

                               sets/grade-6/gcf.csv
                               sets/grade-6/lcm.csv

CSV format conventions (consistent across all 9 files):
  - Header row: column,problem,answer
  - 5 columns (B, I, N, G, O) × 15 problems = 75 per CSV
  - 15 unique answer values per column (cross-column
    repeats allowed)
  - LaTeX formatting: \frac{a}{b}, \times, \div, +, -,
    \operatorname{GCF}, \operatorname{LCM}
  - Fraction answers: improper form, lowest terms, no
    integer-valued answers (the mult/div CSVs skip 1/2 ÷
    1/4 = 2 and similar)

HIGH SCHOOL — all topics (Algebra I, Geometry, Algebra II,
                          Pre-Calculus, Calculus, Statistics)
  Reason: year-2 expansion. Several geometry topics
          (Parallel Lines & Transversals, Circle Theorems,
          Transformations) need diagram support before they
          can be authored. Calculus topics need additional
          time-budget vetting.

DIAGRAM-DEPENDENT TOPICS (defer until diagram support exists)
  Pythagorean Theorem (8.G.7)
    Reason: students benefit from seeing the right triangle
            with legs and hypotenuse labeled.

  Polygon Areas (6.G.1)
    Reason: "height" being perpendicular (not a side) is hard
            to convey verbally; trapezoid two-base setups are
            worse. Triangles, parallelograms, trapezoids all
            want diagrams.

SPECIFIC VARIANT CUTS (still inside middle school)
  Slope "From any form" (8.EE.6)
    Reason: standard-form-to-slope-intercept manipulation
            pushes the 45-second budget.

  Angle Relationships multi-angle figure problems (7.G.5)
    Reason: requires diagrams to communicate the figure.

  Pythagorean find-hypotenuse / find-leg [calc] (8.G.7)
    Reason: irrational answers need curated decimal pools;
            year-2 work. (Parent topic also deferred — see
            DIAGRAM-DEPENDENT TOPICS above.)

  Systems of Equations (8.EE.8)
    Reason: deferred to year 2.

  Volume: Cylinders, Cones, Spheres (8.G.9)
    Reason: deferred to year 2. Format does fit one line
            ("Cylinder: r=3, h=5. Volume?" → 45π).

DROPPED FROM v2 (will not return as currently scoped)
